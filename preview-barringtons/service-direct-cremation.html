Understanding Direct Cremation

Direct cremation, also known as unattended cremation, is an increasingly popular choice for families in Liverpool and across Merseyside. This simple, dignified option provides a respectful farewell without the formality of a traditional funeral service.

At Barrington's Funerals, we understand that not everyone wants or needs a traditional ceremony. Direct cremation allows families to say goodbye in their own way, perhaps holding a private memorial service at a later date when they feel ready.

Why Choose Direct Cremation?

Simplicity and Dignity

Direct cremation offers a simple yet dignified way to say goodbye. Without the pressure of organizing a formal service, families can focus on grieving and supporting each other during this difficult time.

Many families choose to hold a memorial service or celebration of life at a later date, in a location that was meaningful to their loved one.

Environmental Considerations

For those concerned about their environmental impact, direct cremation can be a more eco-friendly choice. Without embalming or elaborate ceremonies, it reduces the carbon footprint of the funeral process.

We can also arrange for ashes to be scattered in our memorial garden or at a location of your choosing, subject to permissions.

The Direct Cremation Process

Step 1: Initial Contact

Contact our team 24/7 to arrange the collection of your loved one. We'll guide you through the necessary paperwork and answer any questions you may have.

Step 2: Care and Preparation

Your loved one will be brought into our care and kept in our private chapel of rest. You're welcome to visit if you wish, though this is entirely optional.

Step 3: Cremation

The cremation takes place at a time arranged by us, without attendees. Your loved one is treated with the utmost dignity and respect throughout.

Step 4: Return of Ashes

Ashes are typically ready for collection within 7-10 days. We can arrange for collection from our offices or delivery to your home.

Memorial Options

Just because you choose direct cremation doesn't mean you can't celebrate your loved one's life. Many families find comfort in arranging their own memorial service.

Popular Memorial Ideas
