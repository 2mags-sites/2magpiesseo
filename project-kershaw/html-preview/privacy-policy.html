Privacy Policy

How we protect and use your personal information

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

Arthur Kershaw Funeral Services is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring your personal information is handled safely and responsibly.

This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information about you when you use our services or visit our website. We comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Last updated: January 2024

Your Privacy Protected

Information We Collect

Personal Information

When arranging funeral services, we may collect:

  • Names and contact details of the deceased and next of kin
  • Addresses and telephone numbers
  • Email addresses
  • Date of birth and death certificates
  • Religious or cultural preferences
  • Payment and financial information

Website Information

When you visit our website, we may collect:

  • IP addresses and browser information
  • Pages visited and time spent on site
  • Information submitted through contact forms

Data Collection

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide funeral services - Arrange and conduct funeral services as requested
  • Legal compliance - Complete statutory requirements and death registration
  • Communication - Contact you about arrangements and provide updates
  • Financial processing - Process payments and manage accounts
  • Service improvement - Understand how to improve our services
  • Memorial services - Provide ongoing memorial and remembrance services

We will never sell your personal information to third parties or use it for marketing without your explicit consent.

Responsible Use

Who We Share Information With

We may share information with:

  • Statutory authorities - Registrars, coroners, and government departments as legally required
  • Service providers - Crematoriums, cemeteries, churches, and celebrants involved in the funeral
  • Third-party suppliers - Florists, memorial masons, and other suppliers you've chosen
  • Medical professionals - Doctors for cremation certificates where required
  • Payment processors - Secure payment processing services

We only share the minimum information necessary and ensure all third parties respect your privacy.

Trusted Partners Only

How We Protect Your Information

Security Measures

We take the security of your personal information seriously and have implemented appropriate measures including:

  • Secure storage systems with restricted access
  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Regular staff training on data protection
  • Secure disposal of physical and digital records
  • Regular security assessments and updates

Retention Period

We retain personal information only as long as necessary:

  • Funeral arrangement records - 10 years
  • Financial records - 7 years
  • Website enquiries - 2 years

Secure & Protected

Your Rights

Under data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Access - Request copies of your personal information
  • Rectification - Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Erasure - Request deletion of your information (subject to legal requirements)
  • Restriction - Request restriction of processing
  • Portability - Request transfer of your data to another organization
  • Object - Object to certain types of processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond within one month.
