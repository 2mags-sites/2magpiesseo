Expert Guidance

General Funeral Questions

What should I do immediately after someone dies?

Call us first on 0161 969 2288 - we're available 24/7. If the death was expected, call the GP or district nurse. If unexpected, call 999. We'll guide you through everything else, including collecting your loved one and starting the arrangements. Don't worry about anything else until we've spoken.

How much does a funeral cost?

Funeral costs vary greatly depending on your choices. Our services range from £995 for direct cremation to over £5,000 for elaborate traditional funerals. Most families spend £2,500-£3,500. We provide detailed written quotes with no hidden charges and can work with most budgets. See our detailed pricing page for more information.

How long do we have to arrange the funeral?

There's no legal time limit, though funerals typically take place 1-3 weeks after death. You must register the death within 5 days, but the funeral can be later if needed for family to travel or for other reasons. We can arrange very quick funerals (within 2-3 days) if required for religious reasons.

Who has the legal right to arrange the funeral?

Usually the surviving spouse or civil partner, then adult children, then parents, then siblings. However, we encourage families to work together. If there are disputes, we can suggest mediation. The person arranging the funeral is also responsible for the costs unless other arrangements are made.

Do I need to use a funeral director?

No, it's not legally required, but it's highly recommended. Funeral directors handle complex legal paperwork, coordinate multiple services, and provide essential equipment and facilities. DIY funerals are possible but require significant time, knowledge, and stress during an already difficult time.

What's the difference between burial and cremation?

Burial involves placing the coffin in the ground at a cemetery, where it remains permanently. Cremation involves the coffin being placed in a cremator, and the ashes are returned to you. Burial typically costs more due to grave purchase costs. Both can have the same type of funeral service beforehand.

Questions About Funeral Services

What happens at a funeral service?

A funeral service typically includes music, readings, prayers or reflections, and a eulogy celebrating the person's life. The format depends on your wishes - it can be religious, humanist, or civil. Services usually last 20-30 minutes at a crematorium, longer in churches. We'll help you plan every detail.

Can we have a service without the body present?

Yes, this is called a memorial service. It's often chosen when the body has been donated to medical science, or when families want a direct cremation followed by their own celebration of life. Memorial services can be held anywhere and at any time after the death.

Who can give readings or speak at the funeral?

Anyone can give readings or deliver eulogies - family members, friends, colleagues, or children. Some people prefer to have the celebrant read their words if they don't feel able to speak. We can arrange microphones and provide guidance on what works well in each venue.

Can we personalize the funeral service?

Absolutely. We encourage families to make the service personal. This might include favourite music, photos, special flowers, particular readings, displaying hobbies or interests, or involving grandchildren. The more personal the service, the more meaningful it becomes for everyone attending.

What should guests wear to a funeral?

Traditional black isn't essential - dark, smart clothing is appropriate. Some families request bright colours if the deceased would have preferred this. The key is to dress respectfully. If you're unsure, darker colours are always safe. Children should be dressed smartly but comfortably.

How long does a cremation take?

The actual cremation process takes 1-2 hours, but this happens after the service when families have left. Crematorium services are typically allocated 30-minute slots, though this can be extended if needed. The ashes are usually ready for collection 2-3 days later.

Practical Arrangements

Where will my loved one be cared for before the funeral?

Your loved one will be cared for in our chapel of rest at our funeral home on Washway Road in Sale. Our facilities are modern, peaceful, and maintained to the highest standards. Family can visit during opening hours, and we can arrange private visits outside these times if needed.

Can we view our loved one before the funeral?

Yes, we encourage this if it will help with your grief. Viewing can provide closure and help make the death feel real. We prepare your loved one with dignity and care. Family visits can be arranged at our chapel of rest, and we can accommodate special requests like bringing items or spending extended time together.

What happens to the ashes after cremation?

Ashes are returned to the family in a temporary container 2-3 days after the cremation. You can keep them at home, scatter them in a special place (with landowner permission), bury them in a cemetery, or place them in a columbarium. We can help arrange any of these options.

Do we need to buy a grave in advance?

No, we can arrange grave purchase as part of the funeral arrangements. However, buying in advance can fix costs and ensure you get a preferred location. Some cemeteries have waiting lists for popular areas. We can advise on options in each local cemetery.

How many people can attend the funeral?

This depends on the venue. Most crematorium chapels hold 80-120 people seated, more standing. Churches vary greatly. Graveside services are less limited by space. During busy periods or for very large funerals, we can arrange for additional seating or webcast services for those unable to attend in person.

What if someone dies away from home?

We can arrange repatriation from anywhere in the UK or abroad. This involves additional paperwork and costs, but we handle all arrangements. If someone dies abroad, we work with specialist repatriation companies and consular services. The process takes longer but we'll keep you informed throughout.

Financial Questions

Do we have to pay immediately?

No, we don't require immediate payment. We understand that funds often need to come from the deceased's estate, which takes time. Our standard terms allow time for this. We'll discuss payment arrangements openly during our initial meeting to avoid any stress.

Can we get help with funeral costs?

Yes, there are several options. The government's Funeral Payment can provide up to £1,000 if you receive certain benefits. Some local charities also help with funeral costs. We can advise on eligibility and help you apply. We also offer various cost-effective funeral options.

What if the deceased had a pre-paid funeral plan?

Pre-paid plans cover most funeral costs, though some extras may still apply. We'll honour any pre-paid plan arrangements and work within the plan's provisions. If you're not sure if there's a plan in place, we can help you check with major plan providers.

Are there any hidden charges?

No, we believe in complete transparency. Our written quotes include all our professional services and itemise third-party costs like crematorium fees. The only additional costs would be extras you choose to add later, and we'll always discuss these with you first.

Religious and Cultural Questions

Can you accommodate different religious requirements?

Yes, we have experience with all major faiths and many cultural traditions. We work closely with local clergy, imams, rabbis, and other religious leaders. We understand requirements around timing, burial methods, preparation of the body, and service formats for different religions.

What if we want a non-religious funeral?

Absolutely fine - we arrange many non-religious funerals. You can choose a humanist celebrant, a civil celebrant, or have family members conduct the service themselves. The focus is on celebrating the person's life and achievements rather than religious content.

Can we have music that isn't traditional hymns?

Yes, most venues can play any music you choose - pop songs, classical pieces, jazz, or anything meaningful to your loved one. We can arrange live musicians if preferred. The only limitation is volume levels at some crematoria, but we'll advise on what works best at each venue.

What about environmental concerns?

We offer several environmentally-friendly options including woodland burials, biodegradable coffins made from cardboard or wicker, and natural burial grounds. Cremation is also considered environmentally friendly. We can discuss all options and help you choose what aligns with your values.
