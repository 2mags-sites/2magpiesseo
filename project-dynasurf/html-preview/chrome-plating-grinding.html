What is Hard Chrome Plating?

Hard chrome plating, also known as industrial chrome or engineered chrome, is an electroplating process that deposits a layer of chromium onto metal components. Unlike decorative chrome, hard chrome plating is applied for its exceptional hardness, wear resistance, and low friction properties, making it ideal for industrial applications.

At Dynasurf, we combine hard chrome plating with precision grinding to restore worn components to their original dimensions or enhance them beyond factory specifications. This process is particularly effective for steering components, gearbox parts, and engine components that have suffered wear, corrosion, or damage.

Our chrome plating thickness ranges from 0.025mm to 0.5mm, depending on the application and wear requirements. The resulting surface hardness of 68-72 HRC provides exceptional durability, often exceeding the lifespan of the original component.

Frequently Asked Questions

How thick can you apply chrome plating? ‚ñº

We can apply hard chrome plating from 0.025mm (0.001") up to 0.5mm (0.020") thickness. The optimal thickness depends on the application - thin coatings for close tolerance fits, medium thickness for general wear protection, and heavy deposits for severely worn components or where maximum durability is required.

Can you chrome plate any metal component? ‚ñº

We can chrome plate most ferrous metals including carbon steel, alloy steel, stainless steel, and cast iron. Some non-ferrous metals like copper alloys can also be plated with appropriate pre-treatment. Aluminium requires special processing. We'll assess your component and advise on suitability for chrome plating.

How long does chrome plating last? ‚ñº

Hard chrome plating typically lasts 5-10 times longer than unplated steel surfaces. With proper maintenance and lubrication, chrome plated components can operate for many years. The 68-72 HRC surface hardness provides exceptional wear resistance, often outlasting the original component's service life.

What size components can you handle? ‚ñº

Our plating tanks and grinding equipment can accommodate components up to 2 meters in length and 300mm in diameter. We can handle parts weighing up to 500kg. For larger or heavier components, please contact us to discuss specific requirements and potential solutions.

Is chrome plating cost-effective versus replacement? ‚ñº

In most cases, reconditioning through chrome plating costs 40-60% less than replacement parts. This is especially true for obsolete components, imported parts, or specialised machinery where new parts have long lead times. We provide detailed quotes comparing reconditioning versus replacement costs.

Can you match original specifications exactly? ‚ñº

Yes, through the combination of chrome plating and precision grinding, we can restore components to exact original dimensions within ¬±0.005mm tolerance. We can also modify dimensions if required, for example, to accommodate oversize bearings or seals. All work is verified with precision measuring equipment.
