Understanding Your MOT Failure Report

Types of MOT Failures

MOT failures are categorised to help you understand their severity and urgency:

  • Dangerous defects: Vehicle cannot be driven until repaired
  • Major defects: Vehicle fails MOT and repairs needed for retest
  • Minor defects: Advisory items that should be monitored or repaired soon

Repair Priorities

We help you prioritise repairs based on safety and legal requirements. Dangerous and major defects must be fixed before your vehicle can pass its retest, while minor defects can often wait until your next service or MOT.

Our technicians will explain each failure point clearly and help you make informed decisions about which repairs to complete immediately and which can be scheduled for later.

Frequently Asked Questions About MOT Repairs

How long do I have to get MOT repairs done?

You have 10 working days from your MOT test to complete repairs and return for a free retest. If you come back within this timeframe, you'll only need a partial retest focusing on the items that were repaired, not a full MOT test.

Can I drive my car if it fails its MOT?

This depends on the type of failure. If your vehicle has 'dangerous' defects, it cannot be driven at all. For 'major' defects, you can only drive to have repairs done or to another MOT test centre. You cannot drive for any other purpose without a valid MOT certificate.

Do you guarantee your repair work?

Yes, we provide guarantees on all parts and labour. Additionally, when repairs are completed by us, your retest is completely free. If any issues remain after repair, we'll fix them at no extra charge.

How much do MOT repairs typically cost?

Repair costs vary depending on what needs fixing. Simple repairs like bulb replacement cost from £10, while brake pad replacement might be £80-150. We always provide clear quotes before starting work, so you know exactly what to expect.

Can you repair commercial vehicles and vans?

Yes, we repair all types of vehicles including Class 4, 5, and 7 MOT failures. We stock parts for commercial vehicles and understand the urgency of getting business vehicles back on the road quickly.

What if I only want to repair some of the failures?

You must repair all 'dangerous' and 'major' defects for your vehicle to pass its MOT retest. 'Minor' defects (advisories) don't prevent your vehicle from passing, but we recommend addressing them to prevent future problems.
