Common Warning Lights & Symptoms

Check Engine Light: The most common warning light, indicating engine management system faults, emissions issues, or sensor problems requiring immediate diagnosis.

ABS Warning Light: Indicates faults with the anti-lock braking system, electronic stability control, or related sensors that affect vehicle safety.

Airbag Warning Light: Signals problems with the supplementary restraint system that could prevent airbags from deploying correctly in an accident.

Performance Issues: Poor running, reduced power, excessive smoke, unusual noises, or fuel economy problems often indicate underlying faults requiring diagnostic investigation.

Car Diagnostics Frequently Asked Questions

How much does car diagnostic testing cost?

Our diagnostic service starts from £49.95 for basic engine diagnostics. More complex multi-system diagnosis may cost more depending on the time required. We always provide transparent pricing upfront and often deduct the diagnostic fee from repair costs if you proceed with recommended work.

How long does diagnostic testing take?

Most diagnostic work takes 1-2 hours depending on the complexity of the fault and how many systems need testing. Simple fault code reading may take just 30 minutes, while complex intermittent faults may require more extensive testing.

Can you diagnose warning lights that aren't on now?

Yes, vehicle computers store fault codes even after warning lights go out. We can read stored and pending codes to identify intermittent faults. However, active faults are generally easier to diagnose than intermittent ones that only occur occasionally.

Do I have to have repairs done after diagnosis?

No, there's no obligation to proceed with repairs after diagnosis. We provide transparent explanations of faults found and detailed quotes for any work needed. You're free to take this information away and consider your options.

Can you diagnose all car makes and models?

Yes, our professional diagnostic equipment covers all major vehicle manufacturers including specialist systems for European, Japanese, and American vehicles. We have manufacturer-specific diagnostic tools and technical data for accurate fault finding across all modern vehicles.

What if the diagnostic doesn't find the fault?

While our diagnostic equipment is very comprehensive, some intermittent faults can be challenging to capture. If we can't identify the fault during initial testing, we'll discuss alternative approaches such as extended monitoring or road testing to isolate the problem.
