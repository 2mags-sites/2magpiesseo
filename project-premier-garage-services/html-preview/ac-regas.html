What is AC Regas?

AC regas is the process of removing old refrigerant from your air conditioning system and replacing it with fresh refrigerant gas. This service restores your AC system's ability to cool effectively and efficiently.

Our regas service includes:

  • Complete system evacuation
  • Vacuum testing for leaks
  • Fresh refrigerant gas refill
  • System pressure testing
  • Performance verification
  • Leak detection service
  • AC system health check
  • Cabin filter inspection

R134a vs R1234yf Refrigerants

R134a Refrigerant: Used in vehicles manufactured before 2017. This is the traditional refrigerant that has been widely used for many years and is still perfectly safe and effective.

R1234yf Refrigerant: The newer, more environmentally friendly refrigerant used in vehicles manufactured from 2017 onwards. It has a much lower global warming potential than R134a.

Identification: We can identify which refrigerant your vehicle uses by checking the year of manufacture, VIN number, and system labels. Using the wrong refrigerant can damage your AC system.

Cost Difference: R1234yf is more expensive than R134a due to its newer technology and environmental benefits, but both provide excellent cooling performance.

Signs Your AC Needs Regas

Recognising when your air conditioning system needs a regas can help you maintain optimal cooling performance and prevent more expensive repairs:

  • Reduced cooling: AC takes longer to cool or doesn't reach desired temperature
  • Warm air: Air from vents is not as cold as it used to be
  • Frequent cycling: AC compressor turns on and off more frequently
  • Increased fuel consumption: AC system working harder to achieve cooling
  • Ice formation: Ice appearing on AC components outside the vehicle
  • Time-based: It's been 2+ years since your last regas service

We recommend having your AC system recharged every 2-3 years as part of regular maintenance, even if you haven't noticed performance issues.

Frequently Asked Questions About AC Regas

How often should I have my AC system regassed?

We recommend having your AC system regassed every 2-3 years as part of regular maintenance. However, if you notice reduced cooling performance, it may need regas sooner. Annual checks can help identify when regas is needed.

How long does an AC regas take?

A standard AC regas typically takes 1-2 hours. This includes system evacuation, leak testing, and refilling with fresh refrigerant. You can usually wait while the service is completed.

What's the difference in price between R134a and R1234yf?

R1234yf refrigerant is more expensive than R134a due to its newer technology and environmental benefits. However, both provide excellent cooling performance, and the choice depends on your vehicle's specifications.

Will regas fix all my AC problems?

Regas will restore cooling performance if the only issue is low refrigerant levels. However, if there are leaks, faulty components, or other problems, additional repairs may be needed. We'll diagnose any other issues during the service.

How do I know which refrigerant my car uses?

Generally, vehicles manufactured before 2017 use R134a, while newer vehicles use R1234yf. We can identify the correct refrigerant for your vehicle using the VIN number and system labels.

What happens to the old refrigerant?

All old refrigerant is safely recovered using professional equipment and sent for proper recycling. We follow strict environmental regulations to prevent harmful emissions and protect the environment.
