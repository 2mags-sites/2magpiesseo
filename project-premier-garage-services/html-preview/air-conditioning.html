Signs Your AC System Needs Attention

Reduced Cooling: If your AC is not cooling as effectively as it used to, or takes longer to reach the desired temperature, it may need servicing or repair.

Strange Noises: Unusual sounds when the AC is running, such as grinding, squealing, or clicking noises, often indicate component problems that need professional attention.

Unpleasant Odours: Musty or unusual smells from your AC system can indicate mould growth or other issues that require cleaning and maintenance.

Visible Leaks: Puddles of coolant under your vehicle or visible refrigerant leaks indicate system damage that needs immediate professional repair.

AC System Maintenance Tips

Regular maintenance can help keep your air conditioning system running efficiently and prevent costly repairs. Here are our expert recommendations for maintaining your vehicle's AC system:

  • Regular use: Run your AC system for a few minutes each week, even in winter
  • Cabin filter: Replace the cabin filter annually or as recommended
  • Annual service: Have your AC system serviced annually for optimal performance
  • Check for leaks: Look for signs of refrigerant leaks under your vehicle
  • Clean condenser: Keep the external condenser free from debris and dirt
  • Professional attention: Address issues promptly to prevent expensive damage

Regular AC maintenance not only ensures comfort but also helps maintain your vehicle's resale value and prevents more expensive repairs in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions About AC Services

How often should I service my car's air conditioning?

We recommend having your AC system serviced annually, ideally before the summer season. This ensures optimal performance when you need it most and helps identify potential issues before they become expensive problems.

Why is my air conditioning not cooling properly?

Poor cooling can be caused by several factors including low refrigerant levels, compressor problems, blocked condensers, or faulty temperature sensors. Our diagnostic service can identify the exact cause and recommend the appropriate solution.

How long does an AC service take?

A standard AC service typically takes 1-2 hours, while more complex repairs may take longer. We'll provide you with an accurate time estimate when we diagnose your system and explain what work is needed.

What's the difference between R134a and R1234yf refrigerants?

R1234yf is the newer, more environmentally friendly refrigerant used in vehicles manufactured after 2017. R134a is the older refrigerant still used in many vehicles. We can service both types and will use the correct refrigerant for your specific vehicle.

Can I run my AC in winter?

Yes, and we actually recommend running your AC system briefly each week throughout the year. This keeps the system lubricated, prevents seals from drying out, and helps clear condensation from windows more effectively.

How do I know if my AC system has a refrigerant leak?

Signs of refrigerant leaks include reduced cooling performance, visible oil stains under the vehicle, hissing sounds from the AC system, or ice formation on AC components. Our leak detection service can locate and repair leaks professionally.
