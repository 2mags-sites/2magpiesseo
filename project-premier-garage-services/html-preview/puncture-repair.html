When Punctures Can Be Repaired

Repairable Punctures: Small holes in the tread area (up to 6mm diameter) caused by nails, screws, or similar objects. The puncture must be in the central 75% of the tread area for safe repair.

Professional Assessment: We inspect the puncture location, size, and internal damage to determine if repair is safe. Factors include angle of penetration, tyre age, existing wear, and previous repairs.

Safety Standards: We follow British Standard BS AU 159f guidelines for tyre repairs, ensuring every repair meets strict safety requirements for permanent reliability.

Honest Advice: If a puncture cannot be safely repaired, we'll explain why and provide competitive quotes for replacement tyres. We never compromise on safety for the sake of a repair.

Common Puncture Causes & Prevention

Understanding common puncture causes can help you avoid future problems and maintain your tyres in optimal condition. Most punctures are preventable with proper awareness and tyre maintenance.

  • Road Debris: Nails, screws, glass, and metal fragments from construction or accidents
  • Sharp Objects: Industrial debris, thorns, or deliberately placed objects
  • Kerb Damage: Impact damage from hitting kerbs can cause sidewall punctures
  • Valve Problems: Damaged or deteriorated valves can cause slow air leaks
  • Age-Related: Older tyres may develop cracks that allow air to escape
  • Under-inflation: Low pressure increases puncture risk and causes rapid damage

Regular tyre pressure checks, visual inspections, and careful driving can significantly reduce puncture risk and extend tyre life.

Emergency Puncture Assistance

What to Do When You Get a Puncture

If you discover a puncture, follow these safety steps:

  • Stop Safely: Pull over in a safe location away from traffic
  • Don't Remove Objects: Leave nails or screws in place to minimize air loss
  • Check Pressure: If tyre still has pressure, drive slowly to our garage
  • Use Spare: If flat, change to spare tyre or call for assistance

Temporary vs Permanent Solutions

Emergency repair options available:

  • Replacement: When repair is not safe or possible

Frequently Asked Questions About Puncture Repair

Can all punctures be repaired safely?

No, punctures can only be safely repaired if they're in the central tread area, no larger than 6mm, and there's no internal damage. Sidewall damage, multiple punctures, or large tears cannot be safely repaired and require tyre replacement.

How long does a puncture repair take?

Professional puncture repairs typically take 30-45 minutes including the free safety check. This allows time for proper wheel removal, internal inspection, professional repair, and quality testing to ensure a permanent fix.

How long will a repaired puncture last?

A properly repaired puncture should last for the remaining life of the tyre when done to professional standards. Our repairs follow British Standard guidelines and come with a written guarantee for your peace of mind.

Should I remove the nail or screw from my tyre?

No, leave the object in place if the tyre still has air pressure. Removing it will cause rapid deflation and may make the puncture worse. Drive carefully to our garage or call for assistance if the tyre is completely flat.

Do you guarantee puncture repairs?

Yes, we provide a written guarantee on all puncture repairs. If a properly repaired puncture fails due to our workmanship, we'll fix it free of charge. However, guarantee doesn't cover new damage or misuse of the repaired tyre.

Can run-flat tyres be repaired after puncture?

Run-flat tyres generally cannot be safely repaired after being driven on while flat, as this can cause internal structural damage. We'll assess each case individually and advise on the safest option for your specific situation.
