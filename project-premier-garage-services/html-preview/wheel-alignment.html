What is Wheel Alignment?

Wheel alignment, also known as tracking, refers to the adjustment of a vehicle's suspension angles to ensure all wheels are pointing in the correct direction. These precise angles affect how your tyres make contact with the road surface.

Key alignment angles we measure and adjust:

  • Toe: Whether wheels point inward or outward when viewed from above
  • Camber: Inward or outward tilt of wheels when viewed from front/rear
  • Caster: Forward or backward tilt of steering axis
  • Thrust angle: Direction rear wheels point relative to centreline
  • Setback: Difference in position between left and right wheels
  • Wheelbase: Distance between front and rear axles

Signs Your Vehicle Needs Wheel Alignment

Uneven Tyre Wear: If your tyres are wearing more on one side than the other, or showing irregular wear patterns, this is often a clear sign that your wheels need alignment.

Vehicle Pulling: If your car pulls to one side when driving on straight, level roads, or the steering wheel is off-centre when driving straight, alignment adjustment is needed.

Steering Issues: Vibrating steering wheel, loose steering feel, or difficulty maintaining straight-line driving all indicate potential alignment problems.

After Impact: Following pot hole damage, kerb strikes, or minor collisions, wheel alignment should be checked as these impacts can knock wheels out of alignment.

What's the difference between 2-wheel and 4-wheel alignment?

2-wheel alignment only adjusts the front wheels, while 4-wheel alignment measures and adjusts all four wheels. Modern vehicles with independent rear suspension require 4-wheel alignment for optimal performance. We recommend 4-wheel alignment for the most comprehensive service.

Can you align wheels if my suspension needs repair?
