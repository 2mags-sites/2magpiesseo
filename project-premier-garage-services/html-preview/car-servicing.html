Service Schedules & Manufacturer Requirements

When Does Your Car Need Servicing?

Service intervals vary by manufacturer, vehicle age, and driving conditions. We recommend:

  • New Cars: Follow manufacturer schedule (usually 12 months/12,000 miles)
  • Older Vehicles: More frequent servicing may be beneficial (6-12 months)
  • High Mileage Drivers: Consider interim services between main services
  • City Driving: More frequent oil changes due to stop-start conditions

Frequently Asked Questions About Car Servicing

How often should I service my car?

Most manufacturers recommend annual servicing or every 10,000-15,000 miles, whichever comes first. However, older vehicles, high-mileage drivers, or those doing lots of city driving may benefit from more frequent servicing. We can advise on the best schedule for your specific vehicle and driving patterns.

Will servicing my car elsewhere affect my warranty?

No, as long as the service is carried out by a qualified technician using appropriate parts and following manufacturer guidelines. We maintain detailed service records and use genuine or OEM equivalent parts to ensure your warranty remains valid.

What's the difference between a basic and full service?

A basic service covers essential items like oil and filter change, basic safety checks, and fluid top-ups. A full service is more comprehensive, including detailed inspections of brakes, suspension, timing belt, and all major systems. We'll recommend the most appropriate service based on your vehicle's age, mileage, and service history.

What happens if you find problems during the service?
