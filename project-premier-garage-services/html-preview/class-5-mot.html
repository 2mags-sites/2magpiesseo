What Vehicles Need Class 5 MOT Testing?

Class 5 MOT testing is specifically designed for private passenger vehicles that can carry 13 or more people (including the driver). This specialised test ensures these vehicles meet enhanced safety standards required for multi-passenger transport.

Vehicles requiring Class 5 MOT include:

  • Minibuses (13-16 seat private passenger vehicles)
  • Large motorhomes and motor caravans with 13+ seats
  • Private hire vehicles with 13+ passenger capacity
  • Community transport vehicles
  • School transport vehicles (private operators)
  • Hotel and venue shuttle buses
  • Private coach services
  • Converted passenger vehicles over 12 seats

Enhanced Class 5 MOT Inspection Standards

Class 5 MOT testing includes all standard vehicle checks plus additional safety requirements specific to passenger-carrying vehicles. Our qualified testers follow strict DVSA guidelines to ensure your vehicle meets the enhanced safety standards required for multi-passenger transport.

Additional Class 5 inspection areas include:

  • Enhanced passenger door and emergency exit checks
  • Passenger seat belt and restraint systems
  • Interior safety equipment and first aid provisions
  • Wheelchair accessibility equipment (if fitted)
  • Driver visibility and mirror positioning
  • Passenger compartment lighting and ventilation
  • Enhanced braking system performance testing
  • Structural integrity for passenger safety

These additional checks ensure your passengers travel safely and that you meet all legal requirements for operating passenger vehicles.

Common Class 5 MOT Considerations

Class 5 vehicles face unique challenges due to their size, weight, and passenger-carrying responsibilities. Our experience with minibus and large passenger vehicle testing has identified these common areas of attention:

  • Brake performance: Enhanced braking requirements due to passenger load and vehicle weight
  • Door mechanisms: All passenger doors must operate correctly and safely
  • Emergency exits: Proper function and accessibility of emergency escape routes
  • Seat belt systems: All passenger restraints must be secure and functional
  • Suspension systems: Handling additional weight and ensuring passenger comfort
  • Lighting systems: Enhanced visibility requirements for larger vehicles

Regular maintenance and pre-MOT inspections are particularly important for passenger vehicles to ensure safety and compliance.

Class 5 MOT Requirements & Compliance

Preparing Your Passenger Vehicle

Class 5 vehicles require additional preparation due to their enhanced safety requirements:

  • Ensure all passenger doors operate smoothly
  • Check emergency exit signs and lighting
  • Verify all seat belts are secure and functional
  • Test driver and passenger mirrors for visibility
  • Ensure first aid equipment is accessible
  • Clean all windows for maximum visibility

Legal Requirements

Operating a passenger vehicle carries additional legal responsibilities. Ensure you have the correct driving licence category (D1 for vehicles with 9-16 seats), appropriate insurance cover, and any required operator licences for commercial use.

Frequently Asked Questions About Class 5 MOT

What's the difference between Class 4 and Class 5 MOT?

Class 5 MOT is for private passenger vehicles with 13 or more seats, while Class 4 covers vehicles with up to 8 seats. Class 5 includes additional safety checks for passenger doors, emergency exits, and enhanced passenger safety systems.

How long does a Class 5 MOT take?

A Class 5 MOT typically takes 60-90 minutes due to the additional safety checks required for passenger vehicles. This includes inspecting all passenger doors, emergency exits, seat belt systems, and enhanced safety equipment.

Do I need special insurance for a Class 5 vehicle?

Yes, passenger vehicles require appropriate insurance coverage. For commercial use, you'll need public liability and passenger insurance. For private use, ensure your policy covers the vehicle's passenger capacity and intended use.

Can you test motorhomes and converted vehicles?

Yes, we can test motorhomes and converted vehicles that fall under Class 5 requirements. However, the specific test class depends on the vehicle's seating configuration and weight. We'll help determine the correct classification when you book.

What if my Class 5 vehicle fails the MOT?

If your passenger vehicle fails, we'll provide detailed information about all issues found. We can quote for necessary repairs and often complete work the same day. Due to safety requirements, some Class 5 failures may require specialist parts or additional time.

Do you offer collection services for larger vehicles?

We understand that driving larger passenger vehicles can be challenging in urban areas. We can arrange collection for Class 5 vehicles within the Stockport area, subject to driver licensing requirements and vehicle accessibility.
