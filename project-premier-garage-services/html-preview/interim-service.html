When Do You Need an Interim Service?

An interim service is recommended every 6 months or 6,000 miles, whichever comes first. It's particularly important for certain driving conditions and vehicle usage patterns that put extra stress on your car.

  • High mileage drivers: More than 12,000 miles per year
  • City driving: Frequent stop-start traffic conditions
  • Short journeys: Mostly trips under 10 miles
  • Harsh conditions: Dusty environments or extreme temperatures
  • Older vehicles: Cars over 5 years old requiring more frequent attention
  • Business use: Vehicles that cannot afford unexpected breakdowns

Regular interim servicing helps identify potential problems early, extending your vehicle's life and maintaining its reliability.

Frequently Asked Questions About Interim Service

What's the difference between an interim and full service?

An interim service focuses on essential safety checks and maintenance like oil changes and fluid top-ups. A full service includes everything in the interim service plus more comprehensive inspections, additional component checks, and may include items like spark plug replacement and timing belt inspection.

How often should I have an interim service?

We recommend an interim service every 6 months or 6,000 miles, whichever comes first. This is particularly important for high-mileage drivers, city driving, or vehicles used for business purposes where reliability is crucial.

Can an interim service replace my annual MOT?

No, an interim service cannot replace your annual MOT test, which is a legal requirement. However, regular interim servicing helps ensure your vehicle stays in good condition and is more likely to pass its MOT test without issues.

What type of oil do you use in the service?

We use high-quality engine oils from trusted brands, matched to your vehicle's specific requirements. This includes the correct viscosity grade and specification (conventional, semi-synthetic, or fully synthetic) as recommended by your vehicle manufacturer.

How long does an interim service take?

Our interim service typically takes 60-90 minutes to complete. You're welcome to wait in our comfortable waiting area, or we can arrange a courtesy car if you prefer to carry on with your day.

Do you provide a service history record?

Yes, we provide complete documentation of your interim service, including what was checked, any issues found, and recommendations for future maintenance. This helps maintain your vehicle's service history and can be important for warranty and resale value.
