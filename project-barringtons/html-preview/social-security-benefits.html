Benefits and Financial Support

Help when you need it most

During times of loss, financial concerns shouldn't add to your burden. There are various benefits and support schemes available to help with funeral costs and ongoing financial needs. Our experienced team can guide you through the process and help ensure you receive all the support you're entitled to.

Funeral Expenses Payment

A government payment to help with the cost of a funeral if you're on certain benefits or tax credits.

Who Can Apply

You may be eligible if you're receiving one of the following:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Pension Credit
  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Support
  • Universal Credit
  • Child Tax Credit (with gross annual income of Â£17,005 or less)
  • Working Tax Credit with a disability element

What It Covers

  • Burial fees for a particular plot
  • Cremation fees, including the cost of the medical certificate
  • Travel to arrange or go to the funeral
  • The cost of moving the body within the UK
  • Death certificates or other documents

Important Information

  • You must apply within 6 months of the funeral
  • The payment is a loan and may need to be repaid from the deceased's estate
  • Maximum payment varies but typically covers essential costs
  • You must be responsible for the funeral arrangements

Bereavement Support Payment

Monthly payments for widows, widowers, and surviving civil partners to help with household expenses.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Your spouse or civil partner died on or after 6 April 2017
  • They paid National Insurance contributions for at least 25 weeks
  • You were under State Pension age when they died
  • You were married or in a civil partnership when they died

  • You'll need the death certificate and National Insurance numbers

Guardian's Allowance

Weekly payment for someone looking after a child whose parents have died.

Eligibility

  • You're caring for a child whose parents have both died
  • One parent has died and you can't trace the other parent
  • One parent has died and the other is in prison with a sentence of at least 2 years
  • The child normally lives with you
  • You're already getting Child Benefit for the child

  • Paid every 4 weeks
  • Tax-free benefit
  • Continues until the child turns 16 (or 20 if in education)

Local Council Support

Additional help available from your local council for funeral costs and ongoing support.

Public Health Funerals

  • The council arranges and pays for the funeral
  • Available when no one else can pay
  • Simple but dignified service
  • Costs may be recovered from the estate

Discretionary Support

  • Emergency financial assistance
  • Help with essential living costs
  • Crisis loans and grants
  • Each council has different schemes

Council Tax Support

  • Reduction in council tax bills
  • Available for those on low incomes
  • May be 100% reduction in some cases
  • Apply directly to your local council

Other Forms of Support

Charitable Organizations

Many charities provide financial assistance for funeral costs:

  • Turn2us - benefit checker and grant finder
  • Citizens Advice - free advice and support
  • Age UK - support for older people
  • Local community charities
  • Religious organizations
  • Trade union assistance

Insurance Claims

Check for existing policies that may help:

  • Life insurance policies
  • Funeral insurance/plans
  • Pension scheme benefits
  • Employer death-in-service benefits
  • Union death benefits
  • Bank account insurance

Practical Help

Non-financial support available:

  • Advice on dealing with debts
  • Budgeting and money management
  • Housing advice and support
  • Employment support
  • Counseling and emotional support
