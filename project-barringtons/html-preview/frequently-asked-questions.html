Frequently Asked Questions

We're Here to Help

We understand you may have many questions during this difficult time. Below are answers to questions families often ask. If you can't find what you're looking for, please don't hesitate to call us on 0151 928 1625.

Immediate Arrangements

What should I do when someone dies?

If the death was expected and occurred at home, contact the person's GP. If it happened in a hospital or care home, staff will guide you. In all cases, you can call us immediately on 0151 928 1625 for support and guidance through the next steps.

When should I contact a funeral director?

You can contact us as soon as you feel ready. We're available 24 hours a day. There's no rush – take time to be with your loved one if you wish. We can bring your loved one into our care whenever you're ready.

What if death occurs in the middle of the night?

We're available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. You can call us at any time on 0151 928 1625. If you prefer, you can wait until morning – there's no requirement to act immediately.

What documents will I need?

Initially, we don't need any documents to bring your loved one into our care. For registering the death, helpful documents include the medical certificate, birth certificate, marriage certificate, and NHS medical card – but don't worry if you can't find everything immediately.

Funeral Planning

How long after death does a funeral take place?

Typically, funerals take place within 1-2 weeks, but this can vary. Factors include registration completion, venue availability, and family preferences. We'll work at your pace – there's no rush to make decisions.

Can we have the funeral on a weekend?

Yes, many venues offer weekend services, though availability may be limited and costs may be higher. We'll check all options for your preferred date and advise on any additional charges.

What's the difference between burial and cremation?

Cremation involves the respectful reduction of the body to ashes, which can then be scattered, kept, or interred. Burial provides a permanent resting place in a cemetery or natural burial ground. Both can include a full funeral service. The choice is personal and may be influenced by wishes, beliefs, or practical considerations.

Can we have a viewing or visit?

Yes, we have a peaceful chapel of rest where you can spend time with your loved one. Viewings can be arranged by appointment, and we can accommodate specific cultural or religious requirements. Some families find this helpful for their grieving process.

Costs and Payment

How much does a funeral cost?

Funeral costs vary depending on your choices. Our essential funeral starts from £1,795, with our standard funeral from £2,995. We provide clear, itemized quotes with no hidden charges. Third-party costs (crematorium, celebrant, etc.) are shown separately.

When do I need to pay for the funeral?

We typically send our invoice after the funeral and allow 30 days for payment. If you're waiting for funds from the estate or insurance, we can often extend this. We'll discuss payment options during our initial meeting.

Is financial help available?

You may be eligible for the government's Funeral Expenses Payment if you receive certain benefits. The deceased's estate may also have funds available. Some employers, unions, or associations offer bereavement grants. We can provide information about all available options.

Can I pay in installments?

Yes, we offer various payment plans to help spread the cost. We understand this is an unexpected expense and will work with you to find a suitable arrangement. Please discuss this with us – we're here to help, not add to your stress.

The Service

Who can conduct the funeral service?

Services can be led by religious ministers, humanist celebrants, civil celebrants, or even family members. We work with excellent celebrants of all types and can recommend someone suitable for your wishes. You're also welcome to arrange your own officiant.

Can we have music at the funeral?

Absolutely. Most venues have excellent sound systems for recorded music. Live musicians can often be accommodated too. Music choices are entirely personal – from hymns to pop songs, whatever feels right for your loved one.

How long does a funeral service last?

Crematorium services typically last 20-30 minutes, though longer slots can sometimes be booked. Church or cemetery services have more flexibility. We'll ensure there's adequate time for the service you want.

Can we webcast the funeral?

Yes, most crematoriums offer webcasting services, allowing distant friends and family to watch online. This has become increasingly popular and provides a recording for future viewing. There's usually a small additional charge.

After the Funeral

When are ashes available after cremation?

Ashes are usually ready for collection within 2-3 working days after the cremation. We can collect them for you and keep them safely until you're ready, or help arrange for their scattering or interment.

What can we do with the ashes?

Options include scattering in a meaningful place (with permission if needed), interment in a cemetery or crematorium garden, keeping them at home in an urn, or dividing them among family members. Some choose to incorporate ashes into jewelry or other keepsakes.

How do we organize a headstone?

Headstones can usually be placed after 6-12 months, once the ground has settled. We work with skilled memorial masons and can arrange this for you. Regulations vary between cemeteries, and we'll guide you through the requirements and options.

Is aftercare support available?

Yes, our care doesn't end after the funeral. We provide bereavement support information and can connect you with counseling services and support groups. We're always here if you need someone to talk to or have questions weeks or months later.

Special Circumstances

What if the coroner is involved?

Coroner involvement is routine in certain circumstances and doesn't imply wrongdoing. It may delay funeral arrangements slightly. We liaise with the coroner's office regularly and will keep you informed throughout the process.

Can we bring someone home who died abroad?

Yes, we can arrange repatriation from anywhere in the world. This involves coordination with international funeral directors, embassies, and airlines. While complex, we handle all arrangements and keep you informed throughout.

What about funeral plans or insurance policies?

If your loved one had a funeral plan or insurance policy, bring the documents to our meeting. We work with all major plan providers and will handle the claims process for you, ensuring all specified wishes are fulfilled.

Still Have Questions?

Every situation is unique, and we understand you may have specific concerns not covered here. Please don't hesitate to contact us – we're here to help with any questions, no matter how small they might seem.
