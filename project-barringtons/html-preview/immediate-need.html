When Someone Dies: Your First Steps

We understand this is an overwhelming time. Take a deep breath – you don't have to do everything at once, and we're here to help you through each step.

Step 1

Take a Moment

If the death has just occurred, there's no immediate rush. Take time to be with your loved one if you wish. When you're ready, move to the next steps.

Step 2

Call for Help

Call us first at 0151 928 1625 – we have a funeral director available at all times. We'll guide you through everything that needs to happen and take as much of the burden as possible, allowing you time to collect your thoughts.

Step 3

Medical Certification

A doctor will need to certify the death. Since September 2024:

  • The doctor who treated your loved one will complete a Medical Certificate of Cause of Death
  • A Medical Examiner will review this and call you to discuss any concerns
  • Once approved, the information goes to the Registrar
Step 4

Register the Death

You'll need to make an appointment at the Register Office. Don't worry if you can't do this immediately.

What to bring (if available):

  • Birth certificate or passport of your loved one
  • Their medical card
  • Details about their occupation and pension
Step 5

Special Circumstances

If the death was unexpected, sudden, or occurred in certain circumstances, the Coroner may be involved. This is routine procedure – we'll support you through this process if needed.

Remember:

  • There's no need to rush
  • We're available 24/7 to guide you
  • You don't have to remember everything – we'll remind you what's needed
  • It's okay to ask for help from family and friends

Your first call to us is often the hardest. Once you've made it, we'll be with you every step of the way.
