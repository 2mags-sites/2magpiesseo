Frequently Asked Questions

When should I start estate planning?

Estate planning should begin as soon as you have assets worth protecting or dependents relying on you. Major life events like marriage, children, significant wealth accumulation, or business ownership are key triggers for comprehensive planning.

Do I need a will if I have few assets?

Yes, everyone over 18 should have a will. Without a will, Hong Kong's intestacy laws determine asset distribution, which may not reflect your wishes. A will also allows you to appoint guardians for minor children and executors for your estate.

What happens to my mainland China assets when I die?

Mainland China assets are governed by Chinese inheritance law, which differs significantly from Hong Kong law. Cross-border estate planning is essential to coordinate succession of assets in both jurisdictions and minimize complications for beneficiaries.

How can I minimize inheritance taxes?

Hong Kong has no inheritance tax, but assets in other jurisdictions may be subject to estate taxes. Strategic planning including trust structures, gift strategies, and residence planning can minimize overall tax exposure for cross-border families.

Should I use trusts for estate planning?

Trusts offer significant advantages for asset protection, tax planning, and flexible wealth transfer. They're particularly valuable for wealthy families, business owners, and those with cross-border assets. The suitability depends on your specific circumstances and objectives.

How often should I review my estate plan?

Review your estate plan every 3-5 years or after major life events such as marriage, divorce, births, deaths, significant wealth changes, or residence changes. Regular reviews ensure your plan remains current and effective.
