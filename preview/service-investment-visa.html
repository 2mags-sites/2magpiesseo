Frequently Asked Questions

What are the investment requirements for Hong Kong residency?

Investment requirements vary by scheme. QMAS is points-based with no investment requirement. Entrepreneur visas typically require HK$2-5 million in business investment. The Capital Investment Entrant Scheme (currently suspended) previously required HK$30 million minimum investment.

How long does QMAS application take?

QMAS applications typically take 9-12 months from submission to final approval. This includes initial assessment (6 months), interview (if required), and final vetting. Strong applications with comprehensive documentation may process faster.

Can I include my family in investment visa applications?

Yes, most investment immigration schemes allow inclusion of spouse and unmarried dependent children. Family members receive dependent visas with similar rights, including education access and eventual permanent residency eligibility.

What business sectors are favored for investment visas?

Hong Kong prioritizes innovation, technology, financial services, and businesses contributing to economic diversification. Startups in AI, fintech, biotech, and green technology receive favorable consideration. Traditional trading and service businesses are also acceptable.

What are the ongoing obligations for investment visa holders?

Obligations include maintaining the investment, operating the business actively, complying with tax and regulatory requirements, and demonstrating continued residence in Hong Kong. Regular reporting and renewal applications are required.

How do I qualify for QMAS achievement-based stream?

Achievement-based stream requires exceptional achievement in specific fields including academics, business/commercial, arts/culture, sports, or innovation/technology. Evidence of international recognition, awards, or outstanding contributions is essential.
