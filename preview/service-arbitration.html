Frequently Asked Questions

Why choose Hong Kong for international arbitration?

Hong Kong offers world-class arbitration facilities, experienced arbitrators, supportive legal framework, and strategic location connecting East and West. The city has no nationality restrictions on arbitrators and counsel, ensuring truly international proceedings.

How long does arbitration typically take?

HKIAC cases average 12-18 months from filing to award. Complex multi-party disputes may take 24-36 months, while expedited procedures can resolve matters in 6-9 months. Timeline depends on case complexity and parties' cooperation.

What are the costs of arbitration?

Arbitration costs include institutional fees, arbitrator fees, legal fees, and administrative expenses. HKIAC fees are competitive globally and costs are often lower than multi-jurisdictional litigation. We provide detailed cost estimates and budgets.

How enforceable are arbitral awards?

Arbitral awards are enforceable in 170+ countries under the New York Convention. Hong Kong awards benefit from strong enforcement treaties with mainland China and global recognition, making them highly valuable for international disputes.

Can arbitration proceedings be kept confidential?

Yes, arbitration is inherently confidential. Proceedings, documents, and awards remain private unless parties agree otherwise. This confidentiality advantage over court litigation protects commercial relationships and sensitive information.

What interim measures are available in arbitration?

Arbitral tribunals and emergency arbitrators can grant interim measures including asset freezing orders, security for costs, document preservation, and anti-suit injunctions. Hong Kong courts provide strong support for arbitration-related interim relief.
