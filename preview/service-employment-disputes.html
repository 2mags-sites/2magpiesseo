Frequently Asked Questions

What constitutes wrongful dismissal in Hong Kong?

Wrongful dismissal occurs when termination breaches contract terms, proper notice periods, or statutory requirements. This includes dismissal without cause, failure to pay severance, or termination in violation of Employment Ordinance protections.

How long do I have to file an employment claim?

Most employment claims must be filed within 3 months of termination or the discriminatory act. Discrimination claims have a 12-month limit. However, some contractual claims may have longer limitation periods, so early legal advice is crucial.

What compensation am I entitled to upon termination?

Entitlements include notice pay or payment in lieu, accrued vacation, severance payment (after 24 months), and long service payment (after 5 years). Additional contractual benefits may apply depending on your employment agreement.

Can my employer enforce a non-compete clause?

Non-compete clauses must be reasonable in scope, duration, and geography to be enforceable. Courts consider the employer's legitimate business interests versus the employee's right to earn a living. Garden leave may be required for enforceability.

What should I do if facing workplace discrimination?

Document incidents, report through internal channels if safe, and seek legal advice promptly. You may file complaints with the Equal Opportunities Commission or pursue civil proceedings. Early intervention often leads to better outcomes.

How much do employment disputes typically cost?

Costs vary widely based on complexity and forum. Labour Tribunal cases have minimal court fees but legal costs apply. High Court matters involve higher costs but may justify this for substantial claims. We provide detailed cost estimates and offer flexible fee arrangements.
