Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum amount needed to establish a trust?

While there's no legal minimum, practical considerations suggest at least HK$10-20 million to justify trust setup and ongoing costs. The amount depends on trust complexity, jurisdiction, and family objectives. We can advise on cost-effective alternatives for smaller estates.

How do I choose the right trust jurisdiction?

Jurisdiction selection depends on factors including tax considerations, asset protection features, regulatory environment, political stability, and family connections. We analyze these factors to recommend optimal jurisdictions for your specific circumstances.

What are the ongoing costs of maintaining a trust?

Ongoing costs include trustee fees (typically 0.5-2% of assets annually), legal and accounting fees, investment management costs, and regulatory compliance expenses. Total costs usually range from 1-3% annually depending on trust complexity and services required.

Can I change trust terms after establishment?

Trust modification depends on powers reserved by the settlor and provisions in the trust deed. Many modern trusts include powers of amendment, appointment, and removal to maintain flexibility. Courts may also approve variations in certain circumstances.

How are trust distributions taxed?

Tax treatment varies by jurisdiction and beneficiary residence. Hong Kong generally doesn't tax trust income, but beneficiaries may be taxable on distributions in their residence jurisdiction. Proper structuring can optimize overall tax efficiency.

What happens if the trustee becomes problematic?

Trust deeds typically include trustee removal and replacement provisions. Protectors or beneficiaries may have removal powers, and courts can remove trustees for breach of duty. Professional trustee succession planning is essential for long-term trust success.
