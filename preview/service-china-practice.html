Frequently Asked Questions

What are the advantages of setting up a WFOE?

WFOEs offer complete foreign ownership, operational control, and IP protection. They provide greater flexibility than joint ventures and can engage in most business activities permitted to foreign investment under China's Negative List.

How long does WFOE setup typically take?

WFOE establishment typically takes 3-6 months depending on the business scope, location, and regulatory approvals required. Free Trade Zones often offer faster processing times and simplified procedures.

What are China's foreign investment restrictions?

China's Negative List specifies sectors where foreign investment is prohibited or restricted. Most sectors are now open to foreign investment, but some industries require Chinese partners or have ownership limitations.

How can I protect my IP in China?

Register patents, trademarks, and copyrights in China as early as possible. Consider trade secret protection, employment agreements with confidentiality clauses, and strategic technology transfer structures to minimize IP risks.

What are the key compliance requirements for foreign companies?

Key compliance areas include corporate governance, tax reporting, labor law compliance, data protection, foreign exchange regulations, and sector-specific requirements. Regular compliance audits are recommended.

How are disputes resolved in China?

Options include Chinese court litigation, CIETAC arbitration, mediation, or Hong Kong arbitration with mainland enforcement. Choice depends on contract terms, dispute nature, and enforcement considerations.
