Permanent Residency Requirements

Key requirements for Hong Kong permanent residency and right of abode:

7-Year Continuous Residence

You must have ordinarily resided in Hong Kong for a continuous period of not less than 7 years and have taken Hong Kong as your place of permanent residence.

Ordinary Residence Status

Continuous ordinary residence means living in Hong Kong legally and habitually, with Hong Kong as your principal place of residence.

Absence Limitations

Short absences for business, study, or vacation are acceptable, but extended periods away may break continuity of residence.

Documentation Requirements

Comprehensive documentation proving residence, employment, education, and other activities throughout the 7-year period.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as continuous ordinary residence for 7 years?

Continuous ordinary residence means living in Hong Kong legally and habitually, with Hong Kong as your principal home. Short absences for business, vacation, or study are acceptable, but extended periods away may break continuity.

How long does permanent residency application take?

Right of abode applications typically take 6-8 weeks for straightforward cases. Complex applications with extensive travel history or documentation issues may take 3-4 months.

Can I apply for my family members at the same time?

Yes, spouses and unmarried dependent children who have resided with you can apply simultaneously. Each family member must meet the 7-year residence requirement independently.

What are the benefits of Hong Kong permanent residency?

Permanent residents enjoy right of abode, no employment restrictions, access to public services, voting rights in certain elections, and the ability to sponsor family members for dependent visas.

What happens if I have gaps in my residence history?

We carefully analyze residence patterns and can advise on whether gaps affect your application. Some absences are acceptable, while others may require additional documentation or legal arguments.

Can I lose my permanent resident status?

Permanent resident status can be lost through extended absence (generally 2-3 years) without valid reason, or by establishing permanent residence elsewhere. We provide guidance on maintaining status.
