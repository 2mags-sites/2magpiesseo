Frequently Asked Questions

What security can lenders take in Hong Kong?

Hong Kong law permits various security types including mortgages over real property, charges over personal property, share pledges, account charges, and guarantees. Security must be properly documented and registered where required to achieve priority and enforceability.

How are foreign exchange controls handled?

Hong Kong has no foreign exchange controls, allowing free currency conversion and cross-border fund transfers. However, transactions involving mainland China or other jurisdictions may be subject to their respective exchange controls and regulatory requirements.

What are the licensing requirements for lending?

Banks require HKMA authorization, while non-bank lenders may need money lending licenses. Specific activities like securities lending, consumer credit, or virtual asset services have additional SFC or other regulatory requirements.

How is loan documentation typically structured?

Loan documentation typically includes a facility agreement, security documents, guarantee instruments, and intercreditor arrangements. Documentation follows English law precedents adapted for Hong Kong law, with standardized market terms where applicable.

What enforcement options exist for secured lenders?

Secured lenders can enforce through private sale, public auction, court-appointed receivers, or judicial sale depending on security type. Hong Kong courts generally support efficient enforcement while protecting borrower rights and third-party interests.

How are cross-border transactions structured?

Cross-border transactions require careful structuring considering multiple jurisdictions' laws, tax treaties, regulatory approvals, and currency considerations. We coordinate with local counsel to ensure seamless execution across all relevant jurisdictions.
