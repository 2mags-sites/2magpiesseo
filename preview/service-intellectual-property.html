Frequently Asked Questions

How long does trademark registration take in Hong Kong?

Trademark registration typically takes 6-8 months if there are no objections or oppositions. The process includes examination (2-3 months), publication (3 months opposition period), and registration (1-2 months). Complex cases may take longer.

What can be patented in Hong Kong?

Patents protect inventions that are new, involve an inventive step, and are industrially applicable. This includes processes, methods, devices, compositions, and improvements to existing technology. Software algorithms and business methods generally cannot be patented.

How do I enforce my IP rights against infringers?

Enforcement options include cease and desist letters, opposition/cancellation proceedings, civil litigation for damages and injunctions, customs enforcement, and criminal prosecution for serious cases. We develop enforcement strategies based on the specific circumstances and commercial objectives.

Should I file internationally or just in Hong Kong?

Filing strategy depends on your business plans and budget. For trademarks, consider Madrid Protocol for multiple countries. For patents, PCT applications provide international filing options. We analyze your commercial needs and recommend cost-effective filing strategies.

What happens if someone opposes my trademark application?

Opposition proceedings involve formal hearings before the Intellectual Property Department. We defend applications by filing counter-statements, presenting evidence, and arguing legal grounds. Many oppositions are resolved through negotiation or amendments without full hearings.

How do I protect trade secrets and confidential information?

Trade secret protection requires implementing confidentiality policies, non-disclosure agreements, employee contracts, access controls, and marking systems. Unlike registered IP, trade secrets rely on maintaining secrecy and have unlimited duration if properly protected.
